What is Sickle Cell Disease (SC)?

Sickle cell disease (SC) is the most common inherited chronic blood disorder that affects millions of people around the world and is most common within the Black community. Throughout their lives, individuals with sickle cell disease can suffer a range of conditions, including acute anemia, tissue and organ damage, terrible pain and even strokes.

Our SC Warrior

Alexandria Smith is a sickle cell disease warrior, advocate, and a current GWSB graduate student who is committed to bringing more awareness to the sickle cell community. She is founder of the HemoG.L.O.W.bin (Giving Life Over Worry) non-profit organization whose mission is to raise awareness for sickle cell disease while promoting health and wellness to the SC community.

Why We Need Your Support

The HemoG.L.O.W.bin Blood Drive with Inova Blood Center will be hosted at George Washington University and will serve as a health and wellness initiative during Black History Month. Blood donations are essential in ensuring health in our communities. Your blood donation could be what sickle cell warriors need to treat their condition. Gather your friends, student organizations, and classmates for a day of giving, awareness, and support for sickle cell disease warriors.
